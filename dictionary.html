Abeam

On a line at right angles to the boat's length.

Aft

Towards the stern, or rearmost end, of the boat.

Ahead

In front of the bow, or frontmost end, of the boat.

Astern

Behind the stern, or rearmost end, of the boat.

Backstay

Runs from the stern to the top of the mast to support the mast. Part of the standing rigging.

Batten

A rod inserted in the leech, or rearmost edge, of a sail to support the sailcloth.

Batten Pocket

A pocket in the sail that holds the batten in place.

Beam

The widest line across the boat from right to left.

Beam Reach

When a boat travels at 90º to the oncoming wind, at which the boom is eased about halfway out. The fastest point of sail.

Bearing Away

Turning away from the wind.

Block

The name for a pulley on a sailboat.

Bolt Rope

A reinforcing rope sewn into the edge of a sail to be inserted into grooves on the mast and boom.

Boom

A horizontal spar to which the foot of the mainsail is attached. Gives the mainsail shape, allows it to be positioned to the wind, and can be used to adjust tension in the leech, or rearmost edge of the sail.

Boom Topping Lift

A line that holds the boom up off the deck.

Boom Vang

A block and tackle system that adjusts the vertical angle of the boom, which affects the tension in the mainsail's leech.

Bow

The frontmost end of the boat.

Broad Reach

When a sailboat travels at 135º to the oncoming wind, such that the wind comes from nearly behind the sailboat. The sails are nearly all the way out.

Cabin

The area below deck.

Chainplate

A strong metal fitting that holds a shroud to the deck.

Cleat

A device designed to secure a line. Types of cleats include horn cleats, cam cleats, jam cleats, and ham cleats.

Clew

The bottom, aftmost corner of a sail.

Close Hauled

When a sailboat travels at 45º off the oncoming wind. The sails are trimmed all the way in. This is the closest towards the wind a boat can sail without the sails collapsing.
